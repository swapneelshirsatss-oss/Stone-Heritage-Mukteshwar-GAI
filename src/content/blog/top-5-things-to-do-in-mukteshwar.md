When the monsoon clouds clear and the crisp winter air rolls into the Kumaon hills, Mukteshwar transforms into a wonderland. The views of the Himalayas become impossibly sharp, the pine forests grow quiet, and the scent of woodsmoke fills the valleys. 

If you are planning a winter getaway to [our heritage homestay](/blog/homestay-in-mukteshwar), here are the top 5 things you absolutely must do to make the most of the season.

## 1. Witness the Golden Hour at Chauli Ki Jali

Just behind the Mukteshwar Dham Temple lies [Chauli Ki Jali](https://uttarakhandtourism.gov.in/destination/mukteshwar), a stunning cliffside outcropping that offers panoramic, 180-degree views of the sprawling Kumaon valley. While beautiful year-round, winter sunsets here are legendary. The sky turns deep shades of purple and gold, reflecting perfectly off the snow-capped [Nanda Devi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanda_Devi) peaks in the distance. 

## 2. Enjoy Kumaoni Delicacies by the Bonfire

Winter evenings at Stone Heritage are all about warmth and community. As the temperature drops, we light up our signature outdoor bonfires. This is the perfect time to request authentic local [Kumaoni winter food](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kumaoni_cuisine). Try the *Bhatt ki Churkani* (black bean curry) or *Madua ki Roti* (finger millet bread). These dishes are traditionally designed to keep the body warm in high altitudes.

## 3. Hike to the Bhalu Gaad Waterfall

While waterfalls are generally associated with summer, a winter hike to Bhalu Gaad is incredibly rewarding. The 2-kilometer trek through the forest keeps your blood pumping, and the waterfall itself is much more serene without the heavy summer tourist crowds. The crystal-clear pool at the bottom surrounded by dense greenery is a photographer's dream. Along the trek, you might also spot some incredible [Himalayan winter birds](/blog/himalayan-bird-watching), making it a great dual-purpose hike!

## 4. Stargazing Under the Winter Sky

Because winter air holds less moisture, the night skies in Mukteshwar become exceptionally clear. Turn off the balcony lights of your stone cottage, bundle up in a heavy blanket, and look up. Away from the light pollution of the plains, you can easily spot the Milky Way, shooting stars, and brilliant constellations right from your room. If you are driving up for a [weekend road trip from Delhi](/blog/delhi-to-mukteshwar-road-trip), the sheer contrast of the clear night sky will leave you speechless.

## 5. Do Absolutely Nothing

Perhaps the greatest luxury Mukteshwar offers is the permission to simply slow down. The most popular activity among our guests is sitting on the balcony with a hot cup of adrak (ginger) chai and a good book, occasionally looking up to admire the Himalayas.
